Dear Editor,

We submit our manuscript entitled "Validating Riemannian Parallel Transport Alignment for Cross-Subject EEG Vigilance Estimation" for consideration in IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering.

Cross-subject generalization is a fundamental challenge in EEG-based vigilance monitoring, a core application area of biomedical engineering with direct clinical and occupational safety implications. While Riemannian geometry-based alignment methods have shown promise for BCI classification tasks, their effectiveness for continuous vigilance regression—where the target variable fluctuates at high temporal resolution—has remained an open question.

Our work makes four contributions:

1) We provide the first systematic validation that Riemannian parallel transport alignment significantly improves cross-subject vigilance regression. On 23 subjects under leave-one-subject-out cross-validation, alignment raises the mean Pearson correlation from 0.585 to 0.692 (p = 0.0007, Cohen's d = 0.824), improving 21 of 23 subjects individually.

2) We demonstrate that the benefit extends to practical 4-channel forehead EEG (98.3% of full-setup performance), directly addressing the wearable deployment scenario.

3) Through comprehensive ablations (covariance estimators, regressors, reference points, per-band vs. global alignment), we isolate the performance gain to the Riemannian geometry rather than individual pipeline choices.

4) The computational overhead is negligible (<0.01 s per subject), making the approach suitable for real-time wearable biomedical devices.

We believe this work aligns closely with TBME's scope—bridging rigorous signal processing methodology with practical biomedical instrumentation challenges—and will interest the broad readership of biomedical engineers working on EEG-based monitoring systems.
